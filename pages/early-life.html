Early Life
Early Childhood
Stephen Hawking was born on January 8, 1942, in Oxford, England.
His parents, Frank and Isobel Hawking, were both graduates of the University of Oxford, and his father was a medical researcher.
From an early age, Hawking displayed a passion for science and a knack for mathematics.
University Days
He attended University College, Oxford, where he studied physics and chemistry.
Later, he pursued his Ph.D. in cosmology at the University of Cambridge, where he began to show early signs of his brilliance in theoretical physics.
Eventual Diagnosis
In 1963, at the age of 21, he was diagnosed with ALS, a neurodegenerative disease, which left him with limited physical abilities.
Despite these limitations, his mind remained as sharp as ever, and he continued to make significant contributions to the field of theoretical physics.
